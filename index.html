How it works

How we calculate tranche size

The calculator starts with position sizing — how much of your total portfolio should go into one stock. This depends on how many stocks you own: 1 stock (30–50%), 2–3 stocks (15–30% each), 4+ stocks (10–20% each).

Once you know your max position size, the tranche formula kicks in: Dip percentage × Money you have to spend = Tranche size. We cap it at 20% of the position's current cost. The tranche—a measured portion you buy on each dip—can't push your total position above your personal maximum.

What is deworsification?

Diversification is smart, but over-diversification is 'deworsification'—you end up with more positions than you can properly track, diluting your returns. (This term was coined by Charlie Munger, a famous investor and Warren Buffett's longtime business partner at Berkshire Hathaway). Munger calls for a concentration of individual stock positions you really understand rather than have a lot of different positions for the sake of having a lot of different positions. Before you invest in any company, you need to have a strong thesis, or reasoning, behind investing in the company. The company needs to be one you really understand. Also, it should be one you can keep up with as events and changes happen that could alter your thesis about it.

This is a framework, not advice

This calculator is educational only. It shows you one approach to thinking about position sizing. You make the final decision. Always run your own analysis, understand your thesis, and only invest money you can afford to lose.
